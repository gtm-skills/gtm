# Competitive Intelligence Prompts

Know your competition. Position confidently. Win more deals.

---

## Competitor Research

### Quick Competitor Overview

```
Give me a quick competitive overview of [COMPETITOR]:

1. What they do (one sentence)
2. Target customer
3. Pricing model (if known)
4. Key differentiators
5. Common complaints/weaknesses
6. How they position against companies like mine

My company: [YOUR COMPANY]
We compete on: [DEALS FOR X]
```

---

### Deep Competitor Analysis

```
Do a deep analysis of [COMPETITOR]:

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW
- What they do
- Funding and investors
- Team size and key hires
- Recent news and direction

2. PRODUCT
- Core features
- Recent releases
- Technical approach
- Integration ecosystem

3. GO-TO-MARKET
- Target customer
- Pricing and packaging
- Sales motion (inbound/outbound/PLG)
- Marketing approach

4. POSITIONING
- Key messages
- How they describe themselves
- Customer testimonials themes
- Content strategy

5. WEAKNESSES
- Common complaints (G2, reviews)
- What they don't do well
- Gaps in their offering
- Customer churn reasons

6. HOW TO WIN AGAINST THEM
- Our advantages
- Their vulnerabilities
- Proof points to use
- Positioning strategy
```

## Battlecards

### Build a Battlecard

```
Create a competitive battlecard for [COMPETITOR]:

My company: [YOUR COMPANY]
My product: [WHAT YOU SELL]

Include:
1. QUICK FACTS
- What they do, pricing, target customer

2. HOW THEY WIN
- Where they're strong
- Types of deals they win

3. HOW WE WIN
- Where we're strong
- Types of deals we win

4. POSITIONING
- When to mention them
- When not to mention them
- How to position head-to-head

5. OBJECTION HANDLING
- "Why not [Competitor]?"
- "[Competitor] is cheaper"
- "We're already using [Competitor]"

6. TRAP QUESTIONS
- Questions to ask that expose their weakness
- Features to demo that they don't have

7. PROOF POINTS
- Customers who switched from them
- Competitive win stories
```

---

### Battlecard for Specific Deal

```
I'm competing against [COMPETITOR] for [DEAL/COMPANY].

What I know:
- Prospect situation: [CONTEXT]
- What they like about competitor: [IF KNOWN]
- Decision criteria: [HOW THEY'LL DECIDE]

Create a deal-specific battlecard:
1. Where we're stronger for THIS deal
2. Where they're stronger (and how to neutralize)
3. Questions to ask that favor us
4. Demo moments that highlight our advantage
5. Proof points relevant to this prospect
```

## Competitive Positioning

### Create Positioning Statement

```
Create a positioning statement against [COMPETITOR(S)]:

We are: [YOUR COMPANY]
We help: [WHO]
Unlike: [COMPETITOR(S)] who [THEIR APPROACH]
We: [YOUR APPROACH]
Which results in: [BENEFIT]

Make it:
- Clear and memorable
- Factually defensible
- Not trash-talking
- Under 75 words
```

---

### Position in a Crowded Market

```
My market has many competitors. Help me position:

Competitors and their positions:
1. [COMPETITOR A]: [HOW THEY POSITION]
2. [COMPETITOR B]: [HOW THEY POSITION]
3. [COMPETITOR C]: [HOW THEY POSITION]

My product: [WHAT YOU SELL]
Our strengths: [LIST]

Suggest:
1. A unique position we can own
2. How to describe it simply
3. Proof points needed
4. What to avoid claiming
```

---

### Category Creation

```
Help me create a new category instead of competing in an existing one:

Current category: [WHERE I'M USUALLY PLACED]
Problem with that category: [WHY IT'S BAD]
What makes us different: [UNIQUE APPROACH]

Create:
1. New category name options (5)
2. Definition of the category
3. Why existing approaches fall short
4. Why we're the leader of this category
5. How to educate the market
```

## Competitive Conversations

### Handle "Why Not [Competitor]?"

```
The prospect asked: "Why should we choose you over [COMPETITOR]?"

Context:
- Competitor: [NAME]
- What they like about competitor: [IF KNOWN]
- Our real advantages: [LIST]

Create a response that:
- Acknowledges competitor respectfully
- Focuses on our strengths (not their weaknesses)
- Is relevant to this prospect's needs
- Ends with a clarifying question
```

---

### Handle "They're Cheaper"

```
The prospect said: "[COMPETITOR] is cheaper."

Our pricing: [AMOUNT]
Their pricing: [AMOUNT if known]
Our value: [WHY WE'RE WORTH IT]

Create a response that:
- Doesn't trash competitor's product
- Reframes from price to value/TCO
- Asks questions about what they're comparing
- Provides proof of ROI
```

---

### Competitive Trap Questions

```
Generate "trap questions" that expose [COMPETITOR]'s weaknesses:

Their known weaknesses:
1. [WEAKNESS 1]
2. [WEAKNESS 2]
3. [WEAKNESS 3]

Create questions I can ask prospects (who are evaluating us both) that:
- Seem like normal discovery questions
- Surface concerns about competitor
- Lead them to value what we do well
- Don't seem like competitive attacks
```

---

### Respond to Competitor FUD

```
[COMPETITOR] is spreading this about us:

"[WHAT THEY'RE SAYING]"

Create a response that:
- Addresses the concern directly
- Provides evidence/proof
- Doesn't get defensive or petty
- Turns it into a positive
```

## Win/Loss Analysis

### Win Analysis Template

```
Analyze this competitive win:

Won against: [COMPETITOR]
Customer: [COMPANY]
Deal details: [SIZE, TIMELINE, etc.]

Questions to answer:
1. Why did they choose us?
2. What did they like about competitor?
3. What was the deciding factor?
4. What almost lost us the deal?
5. What can we repeat?

[PASTE NOTES OR FEEDBACK]
```

---

### Loss Analysis Template

```
Analyze this competitive loss:

Lost to: [COMPETITOR]
Prospect: [COMPANY]
Deal details: [SIZE, TIMELINE, etc.]

Questions to answer:
1. Why did they choose competitor?
2. What did they like about us?
3. Could we have won with different positioning?
4. What should we change?
5. Is this a pattern?

[PASTE NOTES OR FEEDBACK]
```

---

### Competitive Trends

```
Based on my recent deals:

Wins against [COMPETITOR]: [NUMBER/DETAILS]
Losses to [COMPETITOR]: [NUMBER/DETAILS]

Identify:
1. Patterns in wins (when do we beat them?)
2. Patterns in losses (when do they beat us?)
3. Positioning adjustments to make
4. Deals we should/shouldn't pursue
5. Product gaps to address
```
